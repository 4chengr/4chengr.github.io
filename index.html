Who Am I?


As I positioned the Starry Night painting under my phone camera, I was ecstatic to see "Style: 93% Vincent van Gogh." ArtMatch, my machine-learning window to the past, was designed to match artistic styles with their famous pioneers. This, however, was just the beginning of my journey in image recognition. Further pursuing this field's far-reaching capabilities at the Sylvester Comprehensive Cancer Center, I gained invaluable experience as a research intern.

I started playing violin at the age of five. In Ohio, I studied with Kurt Sassmannshaus in Ohio and joined the Starling Project at the Cincinnati Conservatory of Music. I have performed at the Schuster Center as concertmaster of the Dayton Youth Philharmonic Orchestra and have since performed with the Los AngelesYouth Orchestra at Carnegie Hall. After moving to LA, I began studying with Joan Kwuon under Colburn Conservatory in 2022. During my High School years I played as concertmaster/lead of the Oakwood and Harvard-Westlake school's orchestra. Within my violin career, I have won first and second prizes in several international competitions.

Every single lesson, Mika brings her quarter-sized violin, her beginner Suzuki Music book, and, without fail, her radiant smile. Each Saturday, it's my privilege to teach her through my school's Plot Twist club, which is dedicated to spreading music to under-resourced and hospitalized children. Siji, the club leader at the time, introduced me to nine-year-old Mika during my sophomore year. While her enthusiasm and talent were apparent from the start, she is one of the many students who have musical passions but lack the resources to see their full potential.
